  • 1992

    Seo Taeji and Boys, considered one of the first KPop bands, debuted in 1992 with song "Nan Arayo," which can be translated to "I Know".

  • 1996

    Legendary boy band H.O.T debuted. They became the first pop group in South Korea to sell a million albums and was also the first group to perform at Seoul Olympic Stadium. This is an incredible feat considering the economic crisis South Korea was going through in the 1990s.

  • 1997

    Rival to boy band H.O.T debuted with the name Sech Kies. Other groups such as Baby V.O.X, NRG, and S.E.S. debuted. This signals the official beginning of K-Pop development.

    Pictured: S.E.S.

  • 2003

    The beginning of the 2nd generation of K-Pop is marked with the debut of TVXQ.

  • 2005

    Super Junior debuts, along with notable soloists like Jessi and Lim Jeong-hee.

    Pictured: Super Junior

  • 2006

    This year marks the start of legendary boy band Big Bang, who still remains relevant to this day, becoming the role model for many people aspiring to be K-pop singers.

  • 2007

    K-Pop groups such as Wonder Girls, KARA, F.T. Island and Girls Generation debuted. K-Pop groups also started to focus on taking K-Pop to other countries. Notably, K-Pop soloist Rain became the first Korean to perform at the Tokyo Dome in Japan.

    Pictured: Girls Generation

  • 2008

    Power vocal duo Davichi debuted, along with SHINee, 2AM, 2PM, and U-Kiss.

    Pictured: SHINee

  • 2009

    The number of K-Pop group debuts started to increase rapidly, signalling the beginning of a K-Pop industry boom.

    Number of group debuts: 15
    Notable debuts: 2NE1, f(x), 4Minute
    Pictured: 2NE1

  • 2010

    Number of group debuts: 13
    Notable debuts: SISTAR, Infinite, Miss A, Girl's Day
    Pictured: Infinite

  • 2011

    Number of group debuts: 19
    Notable debuts: Block B, APink, B1A4
    Pictured: APink

  • 2012

    With the boom of PSY's "Gangnam Style" throughout the world, the paradigm of K-Pop shifted drastically.

    Number of group debuts: 40
    Notable debuts: BTOB, EXO, VIXX, AOA
    Pictured: PSY

  • 2013

    This year marks the official beginning of 3rd generation KPop, where the groups have developed concepts and larger, more intricate fandoms.

    Number of group debuts: 31
    Notable debuts: BTS, AOA Black
    Pictured: BTS

  • 2014

    A tragic accident shook K-Pop fans when group Ladies' Code got into a serious car accident, causing the death of two members.

    Number of group debuts: 50
    Notable debuts: GOT7, Mamamoo, AKMU, Red Velvet, WINNER
    Pictured: AKMU

  • 2015

    Number of group debuts: 54
    Notable debuts: Twice, Seventeen, Monsta X, Oh My Girl, GFriend
    Pictured: Twice

  • 2016

    This year marks the start of K-Pop generation 3.5, which is characterized by the increased power of consumer voice. Audition programs to choose the members of groups were at large, listening to more of what the consumer wants and expects.

    Number of group debuts: 47
    Notable debuts: IOI, NCT, Blackpink, SF9
    Pictured: Blackpink

  • 2017

    More iconic moments in K-Pop happened in 2017, including BTS winning Top Social Artist at the Billboard Music Awards (becoming the first K-Pop band to do so). In addition, BTS was the first K-Pop act to perform on an American awards show, performing their song "DNA" at the American Music Awards.

    Number of group debuts: 76
    Notable debuts: Dreamcatcher, LOONA, Wanna One, K.A.R.D
    Pictured: BTS at the BBMAs

  • 2018

    This year was remarkable for LGBTQ+ K-Pop fans, as the first openly gay idol, Holland, debuted.

    Number of group debuts: 75
    Notable debuts: ATEEZ, Stray Kids, (G)I-DLE
    Pictured: Holland

  • 2019

    K-Pop slowly is breaking boundaries, being unable to be defined simply within South Korea with globalized groups like BTS, Blackpink, ATEEZ, and Stray Kids.

    Number of group debuts: 70
    Notable debuts: TXT, ITZY, AB6IX, Super M
    Pictured: TXT

  • 2020

    Despite the pandemic, K-Pop groups were quite active in releasing new songs and music videos, causing album sales and streams to continue to increase. BTS breaks yet another record by becoming the first all-South Korean group to top the Billboard Hot 100 with their song "Dynamite".

    Number of group debuts: 25
    Pictured: BTS
